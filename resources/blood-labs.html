Blood Lab Tests

Blood Lab Tests and Their Normal Values
Test Name Normal Range What It Measures / Indicates
Hemoglobin (Hb) Men: 130–170 g/L
Women: 110–150 g/L 		Oxygen-carrying capacity of red blood cells; low in anemia
White Blood Cell Count (WBC) 4–11 × 10⁹/L Immune response; high in infection, low in immunosuppression
Platelet Count 150–400 × 10⁹/L Blood clotting ability; low in bleeding disorders
Sodium (Na⁺) 135–145 mmol/L Fluid balance, nerve and muscle function
Potassium (K⁺) 3.5–5.3 mmol/L Heart and muscle function
Urea 3–7 mmol/L Kidney function and protein metabolism
Creatinine 60–120 µmol/L Kidney function
Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR) 90–120 mL/min/1.73m² Overall kidney function
Blood Glucose (Fasting) 4–6 mmol/L Blood sugar levels; diabetes screening
HbA1c 4–6% (20–42 mmol/mol) Long-term blood sugar control
Bilirubin <21 µmol/L Liver function; high in jaundice
ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) 15–45 IU/L Liver cell injury
AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) 15–42 IU/L Liver and muscle damage
ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase) 30–130 IU/L Liver or bone disorders
Albumin 35–50 g/L Liver function and nutritional status
Calcium (Ca²⁺) 2.2–2.6 mmol/L Bone health, muscle function
Phosphate (PO₄³⁻) 0.8–1.4 mmol/L Bone metabolism and energy storage

Test Name Normal Range What It Measures
Glucose 70–99 mg/dL (fasting) Blood sugar levels; screens for diabetes
Calcium 8.5–10.2 mg/dL Bone health, nerve function, and muscle contraction
Sodium (Na⁺) 135–145 mmol/L Fluid balance, nerve and muscle function
Potassium (K⁺) 3.5–5.1 mmol/L Heart and muscle function
Chloride (Cl⁻) 96–106 mmol/L Acid-base balance and hydration status
Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) 23–29 mmol/L Acid-base balance (bicarbonate level)
Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN) 6–20 mg/dL Kidney function and protein metabolism
Creatinine 0.6–1.3 mg/dL Kidney function
Albumin 3.4–5.4 g/dL Liver function and nutritional status
Total Protein 6.0–8.3 g/dL Nutritional status and liver/kidney function
ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase) 20–130 U/L Liver and bone disorders
ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) 4–36 U/L Liver cell injury
AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) 8–33 U/L Liver and muscle damage
Bilirubin (Total) 0.1–1.2 mg/dL Liver function and red blood cell breakdown
Cardiac Markers
Test Name Normal Range What It Measures
Troponin I or T <0.04 ng/mL Heart muscle damage; elevated in heart attacks
CK-MB (Creatine Kinase-MB) <5 ng/mL Heart muscle injury; used to diagnose myocardial infarction
BNP (B-type Natriuretic Peptide) <100 pg/mL Heart failure; elevated in fluid overload
NT-proBNP <125 pg/mL (under 75 yrs) Heart failure; more stable form of BNP
Myoglobin 25–72 ng/mL Early marker of muscle injury, including heart muscle
LDH (Lactate Dehydrogenase) 140–280 U/L Tissue damage; elevated in heart, liver, and muscle injury
hs-CRP (High-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein) <1.0 mg/L Inflammation; used to assess cardiovascular risk
Homocysteine 4–15 µmol/L Cardiovascular risk; elevated levels linked to atherosclerosis
Lipoprotein(a) <30 mg/dL Genetic risk factor for cardiovascular disease
